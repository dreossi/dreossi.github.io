## Introduction

Navigating your career as a computational scientist requires more than just technical knowledge. Well funded big tech vs mission oriented biotech vs independence focused academia offer you differing and unique experiences.

In this post, we will cover the crucial differences between project ownership, functional/cross functional collaboration, managerial roles, data generation, and day to day activities that define these industries. We will also try to cover the wet/dry lab resourcing and how much and what kind of impact your models might produce.

> The views expressed in this post are the opinions of the authors. Please note that there are likely exceptions to each of these topics in each area. Your mileage will vary.

## A Framework for Comparison

Instead of a long-format comparison, we've organized the key themes around four areas:

1. **Collaboration and roles** — Who will you collaborate with and what roles will you play? Team dynamics, project ownership, and cross-functional collaboration.
2. **Data management** — What type of data will you handle and what tools will you use?
3. **Modeling and tasks** — What type of models will you deploy and what impact will they have?
4. **Resources and infrastructure** — What computing and financial resources will support your work?

## Comparison

### Collaboration and Roles

**Project Ownership**

- *Big Tech* — Low ownership; impact depends on project novelty. Teams have redundancy.
- *Biotech* — High ownership; responsibility spans data generation to modeling and inference.
- *Academia* — High ownership; impact is narrower.

**Cross-functional Collaboration**

- *Big Tech* — PMs.
- *Biotech* — Lab scientists, SWEs, BD, PMs, PPMs.
- *Academia* — PhD candidates, postdocs in other labs.

**Role of the Manager**

- *Big Tech* — Career growth, cross-functional collaboration, process management.
- *Biotech* — Career growth; trust in small teams, scientific strategy in larger ones.
- *Academia* — Scientific output management, fundraising.

### Data Management

**Data Generation**

- *Big Tech* — Limited custom dataset generation.
- *Biotech* — High, but focused on specific assay types.
- *Academia* — Limited; dependent on external lab collaborations.

**Data Pipeline & Tooling**

- *Big Tech* — SWE support; high-quality internal/paid tools.
- *Biotech* — Internal SWE teams; some public tools; possible DIY solutions.
- *Academia* — DIY or external APIs; free-tier tools.

### Modeling and Tasks

**Types of Problems**

- *Big Tech* — Revenue-driven; clear KPIs.
- *Biotech* — Biological/chemical exploration; KPIs rarely tied to model performance.
- *Academia* — Maximize in silico performance; regular publishing.

**Day-to-Day Modeling**

- *Big Tech* — Fine-tuning internal models; some R&D.
- *Biotech* — Fine-tuning on lab datasets; incorporate experimental nuances; validate predictions.
- *Academia* — Novel methods development on fixed datasets.

**Impact of a 1% Model Improvement**

- *Big Tech* — High; small gains can significantly impact revenue.
- *Biotech* — Low to medium; small gains have limited downstream effects.
- *Academia* — High; can lead to state-of-the-art results and publications.

### Resources and Infrastructure

**Funding & Financial Stability**

- *Big Tech* — Large, stable.
- *Biotech* — Moderate; stability varies.
- *Academia* — Limited; grant-dependent.

**Compute Resources**

- *Big Tech* — High; cutting-edge resources.
- *Biotech* — Moderate; varies by company.
- *Academia* — Limited; shared resources.

## Summary

Jeff Bezos has a famous framework on reversible and irreversible decisions, arguing that reversible decisions should be made quickly, while irreversible ones require careful consideration. Choosing the right place to work falls somewhere in between. We hope that this post serves as a guide to help computational scientists navigate the various facets of career choices in big tech, biotech, and academia. While these might be helpful, remember that careers are long, and it's normal to make mistakes or pick the wrong path at times. There will always be new opportunities, and in the end, you will be okay.
